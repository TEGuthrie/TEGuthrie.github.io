Frequently Asked Questions

  • What's the largest image you can engrave?

      We have several different laser engravers.
      Our smallest engraver marks an area of approximately 170mm by 180mm (6.7" x 7"), and up to approximately 420 lines per inch. The trade-off with the high resolution is speed. Engraving at 420 lines per inch is slow.
      Our largest laser engraver can engrave an area of 380mm x 400mm (15" x 15.75"), and up to approximately 254 lines per inch. This is also our fastest laser engraver.
      Cutting is a relatively slow process, even on our large laser, and limited to materials approximately 10mm - 12mm thick (0.3" - 0.5").

  • What kinds of materials can you engrave?

      We can engrave all kinds of organic materials. When we say organic we mean things like wood, slate, cork, and leather.
      We can also engrave anodized metal, like aluminum.

  • Can you cut with the lasers?

      Yes. We can cut paper, cardboard, picture framing matboard, wood, cork, and leather.
      Cutting can be a slow process, sometimes requiring multiple slow passes. The thicker and harder the material, the longer it takes to cut it.

  • Can you engrave tumblers or cups, like a Yeti?

      Yes and no.
      Yes: We can engrave tumblers and cups, like Yeti, RTIC, Stanley, etc., as long as they are painted or powder coated.
      No: We can not engrave shiny/polished/unpainted tumblers or cups because it requires a type of laser that we do not have.

  • What materials can not be engraved?

      We can not engrave precious metals like gold, silver, platinum, or metals such as stainless steel, copper, brass, or bronze.
      We can not engrave glass, acrylic, or pmags.
      All of the above materials require a type of laser system that we do not have.

  • How long will it take to get my order?

      It depends on how busy we are and how you are receiving your completed items.
      If we are shipping your completed items via USPS, UPS, FedEx, DHL, etc., that will depend on what service you have chosen, and how busy they are.
      We will ship your order as quickly as we can after we complete the work. In all cases we will let you know when we have shipped your order and provide a tracking number if one is available.
